Organic Planet

Summary of Problem:

The extensive amount of synthetic fertilisers and pesticides used for intensive high-yield farming negatively impacts human and animal health and the global environment, while also poses a risk to bee polinators, soil quality and the sustainability of the environment.

Raman et al(2026) reported that 51% of the food commodities are contaminated with pesticide residues, which can lead to immune suppression, hormone disruption, reduced cognitive function,reproductive anomalies and increased risk of cancer in humans.

Link to SDG's
  • SDG 2 – Zero Hunger

    • Organic farming supports sustainable food production by improving soil fertility and promoting long-term agricultural productivity while reducing dependence on synthetic chamicals.

  • SDG 3 – Good Health and Well-being

    • Reducing the use of synthetic pesticides and fertilisers helps lower human exposure to harmful chemicals, contributing to better health and safer food production.

  • SDG 15 – Life on Land
    •

    Organic farming protects biodiversity by improving soil health, conserving ecosystems, and reducing the harmful impacts of pesticides on pollinators such as bees and other wildlife.

STEM based Solution

Organic farming promotes crop rotation and the use of natural repellents to protect production from pests and improve the soil quality through the use of decomposed organic matter. All of this will enhance soil fertility and long-term environmental sustainability. The different sectors in STEM can support organic farming in different ways. Biology scientists can develop natural ways for pest control and help protect bees from the harmful chemicals used in conventional farming nowadays. Digital technologies can design soil sensors to detect the existence of pests,monitor the soil and collect data to improve the soil health. In addition, online learning platforms and mobile applications can be developed to support education and awareness for people interested in organic agriculture.

Feasibility of Project

The proposed project is feasible because many of the required technologies, such as soil sensors, mobile applications, drones, and precision farming systems are already available and widely used in modern agriculture

Althought the initial cost of soil monitoring systems, drones, and digital technologies may be high, these costs can be reduced through government support, environmental organisations, and farmers funding programmes. In long term, organic farming can lower expenditure on synthetic fertilisers and pesticides while impriving soil health, biodiversity, and crop resilence. These environmental and economic benefits can outweight the initial investment.
